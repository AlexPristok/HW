Wild Animals

  • Tigers

    The tiger (Panthera tigris) is the largest living member of the cat family, the Felidae. It feeds by hunting. It lives in Asia, mainly India, Bangladesh, Bhutan, Nepal, Myanmar, Thailand, Cambodia, Vietnam, Malaysia, Indonesia, China, Korea and Siberian Russia.Tigers are solitary animals.

  • Pumas

    Pumas (Puma concolor), also called cougars, mountain lions, catamounts, or brown panthers, are large wild cats that live in the western half of North America, along with Florida, and most of Central and South America.

  • Monkeys

    Monkeys are tree-dwelling (arboreal) simians. They are in the primate order. Monkeys are intelligent, social animals. Monkeys have a tail, even if it is a short one.

  • Hyppo

    The hippopotamus (Hippopotamus amphibius), or hippo, ancient Greek for "river horse" (Ιπποπόταμος), is a large mammal in Africa that usually eats plants. It is one of only two species in the family Hippopotamidae that are still alive. The other is the pygmy hippopotamus.

  • Interesting facts about giraffes

    1. Giraffes have not only a neck and tongue, but also a tail of impressive size - up to 2.5 meters.
    2. Giraffes are busy eating food for up to 20 hours a day, although an adult animal needs 6 kg of leaves per day.
    3. Although giraffes seem slightly awkward, they are good runners – if necessary, animals overcome up to 55 kilometers per hour. Thus, at short distances, a giraffe can overtake a racehorse (see interesting facts about horses)
    4. The blood of giraffes is very thick. If it were not for this feature, the sharp lowering or raising of the animal's head would be fatal to its brain.
    5. Bulky giraffes are able to jump over obstacles up to 1.85 meters high.

  • Why did dinosaurs go extinct?

    Dinosaurs dominated the planet for about 130 million years, which is 100 times more than a person exists. But 65 million years ago, they became extinct along with most of the marine life and flying reptiles. But why did this happen? There are several versions. The most common version is that our planet collided with an asteroid. This caused a terrible explosion that led to tragic changes not only in the Earth's climate, but also in its geological structure. Other scientists argue that the end of the era of dinosaurs was put by the first predatory mammals that feed on their eggs and cubs. There is also a version that the giants disappeared through a decrease in the water level in the sea or a jump in the magnetic field of the planet. But, what really happened, it is no longer possible to find out.
